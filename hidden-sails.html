Creating challenges with unique identities and approaches

The destroyed pier area.

The crystal mine's tunnel entrance and final room.

  • I wanted each challenge to have a clear identity so they would both draw players to them and help the player remember which challenges they have already completed.
    • As part of this, I made sure players regularly navigate to high vantage points to help them spot their next destination.
    • Climbing up to the sign, which is the closest challenge to the entrance of the area, places the player on top of the roof of the main building, providing a thorough overview of the area.
  • I also wanted all the challenges to require different player abilities to successfully complete.
    • For example, climbing the destroyed sign requires use of the grapple, while making it past the snipers on the docks requires use of the bullet-shield because of the sparse cover.
  • The hidden cave within the destroyed tunnel provides unique challenges and setting that make it distinct from the rest of the area.



Listening to playtest feedback and searching for actionable conclusions

A natural grapple spot, "swinging" across a gap.

An awkward grapple spot, requiring a follow-up jump.

  • During early playtesting, I was getting feedback that spaces felt clunky and were not fun to navigate through.
    • But my question asking was too broad: "Did you enjoy navigating through the level?" So I struggled to take any actionable information from it.
  • I began watching players play, taking note of the specific areas that players liked and did not.
    • It became apparent that among the two types of grapple targets, players enjoyed one, and strongly disliked the other.
    • The awkward grapple spots, which required extra jumps to reach the top of, seemed to be the source of frustration that led to the descriptions of clunkiness.
  • I built a small practice arena with the types of grapple spots players struggled with, and had them spend some time practicing the timings of the follow-up jumps.
    • After players were comfortable with this type of grapple, the areas that were described as clunky became enjoyable, and the complaints about navigation subsided.
    • This showed that the real solution to the problem was polishing the grapple mechanics. (I believe that if the grapple automatically clambers the player onto ledges, then these issues would be bypassed.)
  • My takeaway was the importance of locating the true source of an issue so that accurate actions can be taken.
    • If I had listened only to the initial, vague feedback, I would have remade a significant portion of the level, only to find I was no closer to solving the issue than before.



My plans for the second major area in the level

Early blockout of the resort area.

The view of the resort blockout from the destroyed pier area.

  • This area gives the level its name, a complex of buildings known as the Hidden Sails Resort that will overlook the destroyed pier and beckon the player with intriguing architecture.
    • The central element of the resort is the large boat-conveyor belt that can be seen from almost anywhere in the level. It works as a constantly moving elevator, granting the player access to many layers of the area.
  • I am currently designing this area to expand on the challenges that the player encounters in the destroyed pier.
    • There will be more verticality, and the player will have a lot of freedom with navigating around, through, and over the various buildings.
    • Interior spaces will be much more expansive than the pier's, and combat spaces will further test the player's skill.
